Key Insight: In XOR/parity problems, any single axis-aligned split is uninformative because both halves contain roughly equal numbers of
both classes. Greedy splitting commits to a locally optimal noise feature at the root, leaving the vast majority of data in an impure leaf. A
globally optimal tree would require evaluating split combinations or using oblique splits — both intractable for standard C4.5.
